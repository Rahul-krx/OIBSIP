Marie Curie

1867 - 1934

Pioneer of Radioactivity and Two-Time Nobel Laureate

A Legacy of Discovery

Marie Curie was a Polish and naturalized-French physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize, the first person and the only woman to win the Nobel Prize twice, and the only person to win the Nobel Prize in two different scientific fields.

Groundbreaking Achievements

Discovery of Radium

In 1898, Marie and Pierre Curie discovered radium, a highly radioactive element that would revolutionize medical treatment and scientific understanding.

Nobel Prize in Physics (1903)

Shared with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel for their research on radioactivity, making her the first woman to receive this prestigious award.

Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1911)

Awarded for her discovery of radium and polonium, making her the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields.

Mobile X-Ray Units

During World War I, she developed mobile X-ray units to help doctors treat wounded soldiers on the battlefield.
